StayFresh

StayFresh is a food inventory management  app, keeping things fresh by notifying users before they go bad. Easy recipes based on available ingredients are suggested,  saving time on choosing what to cook. Users can see how much food and money they wasted each month, so they can reduce their food waste.

reseaRch & Definition


In the beginning, our goal was to find a way to help people reduce waste.
We found that people were frequently throwing away fresh foods that had been forgotten and went bad.

User Interviews

User Interviews were conducted to determine why people throw things away, and what was being thrown out.

Survey

The team sent out Surveys to collect additional data in addition to interviews.

Competitor Analysis

A Competitor Analysis was conducted to evaluate existing solutions and to have a standard for benchmarking.

Affinity Diagram

Results are analyzed by converting our interview and survey notes and results into an  Affinity Diagram,

Empathy Map

An Empathy Map is created to help the team get into a mindset of User Focused Empathy.

User Persona

A User Persona is defined from the information gathered in our Empathy map, Personifying the User based on our Data.

The Problem

Busy individuals need access to quick recipes that use up perishable items in their households, and to keep track of how much money in food they have wasted. This will help them see how much is being thrown away,  giving them the opportunity to track and change their habits.

Ideation


Using the "I like, I wish, What if" method, the team Brainstormed ideas and functions for the app.

We then Voted on ideas we thought best adressed the problem.

Selected Ideas were  mapped out on a Feature Prioritization Matrix.

This process provided us with a list of potential solutions ranked by their priority to the design.

Iterations

Two Low-Fidelity prototypes were initially created and tested as solutions to the problem.
These mockups were tested, and successful ideas were carried over into a single prototype.

V 1.0: Low-Fi Prototyping

We created our mockups by identifying which features were crucial to solving the problem, Our ideas were refined into  2 low-fidelity clickable prototypes, then tested to see what worked and what did not.
